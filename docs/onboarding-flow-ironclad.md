# TalkToBro 24-Hour Trial — Ironclad Edition

_The trial is an interview. In 24 hours, Bro must prove it's indispensable._

---

## The 5 Must-Haves (What Makes Them Stay)

Not features. Outcomes. They stay because Bro:

| # | Must-Have | Example | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Demonstrates memory** | Recalls wife's name, asks about her later | Hour 1-4 |
| 2 | **Does real work** | Sends email, creates event, completes task | Minutes 1-5 |
| 3 | **Shows proactivity** | Suggests something user didn't ask for | Hour 2-8 |
| 4 | **Crosses platforms** | Works in WhatsApp AND Telegram | Hour 4-12 |
| 5 | **Creates dependency** | "Oh, I need Bro for this" moment | Hour 8-20 |

**Conversion happens at #5.** Everything before is setup.

---

## The 3 Danger Zones (Where They Leave)

| Zone | Time | Risk | Prevention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Hook** | Minutes 0-5 | No immediate value → leave forever | First win in 60 seconds |
| **The Dip** | Hour 1-2 | Just chat, no action → lose interest | Real work delivered |
| **The Chasm** | Hour 12-24 | Nothing memorable → don't convert | Dependency moment engineered |

**If they survive all three zones, they convert.**

---

## The Message Sequence (5 Minutes to First Win)

### Message 1: The Hook (0-30 seconds)

**Trigger:** QR scan or WhatsApp link opened

**Bro sends:**

> Hey, I'm Bro. 👋
> 
> I handle the boring stuff — scheduling, emails, reminders, all of it.
> 
> What's the thing you keep putting off? Just tell me in your own words.

**Why this works:**
- User describes pain in their own language (psychological ownership)
- Bro listens first, acts second (builds trust)
- Zero technical setup required

**If no response in 2 hours:**
> Still there? If you're busy, I can wait. Or just say "later" and I'll check in tomorrow.

---

### Message 2: The Win (1-5 minutes)

**Trigger:** User describes their need

**Parse → Immediate action:**

| If they say | Bro does |
|-------------|----------|
| "Calendar" / "meetings" / "forgetting calls" | Connects calendar → Shows today's events |
| "Email" / "inbox" / "overwhelmed" | Connects email → Shows urgent items |
| "Reminders" / "tasks" / "forget stuff" | Sets first reminder → Confirms it's locked in |
| Vague/unclear | Asks one clarifying question |

**Calendar example:**

> Got it — scheduling is a pain. Let me connect your calendar.
> 
> [Auth button]
> 
> ✅ Connected. Here's what's ahead:
> **Today:** Team standup 10am, Client call 2pm
> **Tomorrow:** Dentist 4pm
> 
> Want me to remind you before any of these?

**Why this works:**
- **Real work in under 5 minutes** (survives Danger Zone 1)
- Shows actual user data (builds trust)
- Offers specific next action

---

### Message 3: The Tease (Hour 1-2)

**Trigger:** User engaged with first win

**Bro sends:**

> That's one thing off your plate.
> 
> By the way — I can do this across WhatsApp, Telegram, even Discord. Same me, everywhere.
> 
> Want a link to add me somewhere else? Or should we get through your list first?

**Why this works:**
- Plants cross-platform seed (must-have #4)
- Optional — user can decline without friction
- Creates "wait, there's more?" moment

**If user says yes:**
> Same Bro, new chat. I'll remember everything from here.

---

### Message 4: The Memory (Hour 2-4)

**Trigger:** Time-based OR user mentioned something personal

**Bro sends:**

> Quick question: what's something you want me to remember about you?
> 
> Your partner's name, your biggest goal, how you take your coffee — whatever.
> 
> I'm building a memory. The more you tell me, the more useful I become.

**User replies:** "My wife is Sarah"

**Bro, 2 hours later:**
> By the way — how's Sarah doing? You mentioned her earlier.

**Why this works:**
- Demonstrates memory (must-have #1)
- Investment psychology (users who share info are more likely to pay)
- Creates future conversation hooks

---

### Message 5: The Proactivity (Hour 4-8)

**Trigger:** User has been active, has connected data

**Bro sends (unprompted):**

> Heads up — you have that call with [Client] in 30 minutes. 
> 
> Want me to prep you with their last email thread? Or just remind you 5 mins before?

**Why this works:**
- Shows proactivity (must-have #3)
- User didn't ask — Bro anticipated
- This is the "oh, I need Bro for this" moment (must-have #5)

**Alternative if no calendar:**
> Saw it's been quiet for a few hours. Want me to check your inbox for anything urgent? Or set a reminder for something tomorrow?

> Last call. Trial ends in **30 minutes**.
> 
> [Tap to continue]
>

## The Safety Net (Edge Cases)

### The Ghost (No response after Message 1)

- 2 hours: Gentle check-in
- 4 hours: Mark as GHOST, add to re-engagement drip
- 24 hours: "Your trial ended. Want to try again?"

### The Skeptic ("Are you a bot?")

> Yep, I'm AI. But here's the thing — I can actually *do* things, not just chat. Want to see?

### The Over-Sharer (Dumps life story)

> That's a lot — let me focus on what will help most right now. What's the one thing you need handled today?

### Empty State (No calendar/email data)

> You're connected! Your calendar's quiet right now — that's fine. 
> 
> When things pick up, I'll be here. Want to set a test reminder?

### Technical Failure

> Hit a snag on my end. Should be resolved in a few minutes. Your trial time is paused — you won't lose anything.

## Success Metrics (Ironclad)

**Good trial (converts ~20%):**

- 3+ interactions
- Remembers one personal detail

**Great trial (converts ~40%):**
- First win + proactive moment
- 5+ interactions
- References Bro's memory unprompted
- Asks about features/pricing

**Conversion-ready (converts ~60%):**
- Expresses FOMO about losing access
- Sets recurring task (implies long-term intent)
- Tells someone else about Bro
